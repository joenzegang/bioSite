A little bit about myself

My name is Charli Massicka I was born and raised in Bangui (Central African Republic). I developed an early interest in Engineering and Languages while living in different countries. Having a background in different cultures of different countries, I have a curious mind and interest in international cultures. I received my Bachelors Degree in Aerospace Engineering in China as an honor graduate student then I moved to America in 2016.

Some of my activities

When I was in high school and college, I participated in sports such as: soccer, volleyball, basketball, track and high jump. Being part of the leadership in many student organizations provided me the opportunity to contribute to many transactions, including building new restrooms for students, purchase of chairs and tables for the school through a system of fund raising that I created. I also enjoy camping, going to church and spending time with my family.

My military experience

I have 3 years of experience in the U.S Army active duty, which I served inland and overseas, in countries such as Kuwait, Iraq and Korea. I attended Basic Combat Training (BCT) at Fort Sill, Oklahoma and Advance Individual Training (AIT) at Fort LeonardHood, Misouri. I worked in the field as well as in an office support role with strong work ethics and positive attitude. I lead and supervise teams in support of battlefield operations, security assignments, provide technical and tactical training and mentorship to subordinates. I am certified in combat driving and I am honored to have been awarded two Army Achievement Medals, Good Conduct Medals, Tactical and Professional development ribons, Overseas Service medals and among ohers.
